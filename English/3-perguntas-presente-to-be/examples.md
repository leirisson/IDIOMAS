# Questions in the Present — Verb To Be

## How to form a question with "to be"

In English, to make a question you **invert** the subject and the verb:

```
Affirmative:  She  is  happy.
Question:     Is   she happy?

Affirmative:  They are  ready.
Question:     Are  they ready?
```

> Rule: To Be + Subject + complement + ?

---

## Yes / No Questions

| Question            | Short Answer (Yes) | Short Answer (No)   |
|---------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Am I late?          | Yes, you are.      | No, you aren't.     |
| Are you okay?       | Yes, I am.         | No, I'm not.        |
| Is he at home?      | Yes, he is.        | No, he isn't.       |
| Is she ready?       | Yes, she is.       | No, she isn't.      |
| Is it possible?     | Yes, it is.        | No, it isn't.       |
| Are we early?       | Yes, we are.       | No, we aren't.      |
| Are they friends?   | Yes, they are.     | No, they aren't.    |

## More Examples in Context

### At school
- Are you a student? → Yes, I am.
- Is he your teacher? → No, he isn't.
- Where is the classroom? → It is on the second floor.

### At work
- Are you busy? → Yes, I am.
- Is she in the meeting? → No, she isn't.
- Why are they late? → They are stuck in traffic.

### About people
- Who is he? → He is my brother.
- How old is she? → She is twenty years old.
- Are they married? → Yes, they are.

### About things and places
- What is that? → It is a book.
- Where is my phone? → It is on the table.
- Is it expensive? → No, it isn't.
